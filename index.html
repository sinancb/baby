Nursing Themes

  • Patient Focus: Emphasizing patient-centered care.
  • Information Focus: Ensuring proper management and use of patient information.
  • Staff Focus: Addressing the well-being and training of nursing staff.
  • Quality Assurance (QA): Ensuring care meets high standards.
  • Leadership: Developing nursing leadership and management skills.

Issues and Trends Affecting RPNs

  • Issues: Specific challenges such as workload or resource limitations.
  • Trends: Changes in the scope of practice over time.

Canadian Healthcare Practices

  • Hospital: Acute care facilities for treatment of severe conditions.
  • Community (LTC): Long-term care focusing on ADLs (Activities of Daily Living).
  • Rehabilitation: Short-term care post-hospital discharge.
  • Private Sectors: Privately owned healthcare services.
  • Public Institutions: Government-funded healthcare services.
  • Hospice Care: End-of-life care services.

Types of Nurses in Ontario

  • RN (Registered Nurse): General nursing duties.
  • RPN (Registered Practical Nurse): Practical nursing duties with limited scope.
  • NP (Nurse Practitioner): Advanced practice nursing, with prescribing authority.

CNO Code of Conduct - 6 Principles

  • Respect client dignity.
  • Provide inclusive and culturally safe care.
  • Ensure safe and competent care.
  • Work respectfully with healthcare teams.
  • Act with integrity in clients' best interest.
  • Maintain public confidence in nursing.

Key Terms in Nursing

  • Fitness to Practice: Mentally and physically capable of providing care.
  • Duty to Provide Care: Obligation to not refuse care.

Seneca’s Nursing Practice Concepts

  • Connectedness: Consulting and collaborating.
  • Competency: Knowledge and skill application.
  • Health and Healing: Promoting patient independence.
  • Professionalism: Maintaining a high standard of conduct.
  • Critical Thinking: Applying problem-solving skills.

Communication in Nursing

Levels of Communication

  • Intrapersonal
  • Interpersonal
  • Transpersonal
  • Small-group
  • Public
  • Interprofessional
  • Intraprofessional

The Communication Process

  • Input → Message → Behavior/Output

Forms of Communication

  • Verbal
  • Non-verbal

Barriers to Communication

  • Defensiveness, changing the subject, asking personal questions, etc.

Documentation in Nursing

  • Purpose: Communication, patient perspective, standards.
  • Electronic Documentation: Security, PHIPA compliance.
  • Methods: SOAP, SOAPIE, PIE, DAR, narrative notes, etc.

Critical Thinking in Nursing

  • General Competencies: Scientific method, problem-solving, decision-making.
  • Specific Competencies: Diagnostic reasoning, clinical decision-making.
  • Reflective Practice: Importance of reflecting on experiences for improvement.
